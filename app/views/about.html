Hi, I'm Dave Hogge!

Watch me bike, travel and work

Born and raised in Hampton Roads, Virginia I spent most of my early life working on the water with my father who was a Chesapeake Bay Waterman. In college I studied History. But it was the introduction of a personal computer to write my senior thesis that shaped my future.

Over the coming years I worked variously as a Multimedia Author, a Video Game Tester, a Roadie and a Travel Blogger. But my passion is always for developing efficient, maintainable, and scalable front-end systems.

I'm not all business and I do know how to have a bit of fun. I've traveled the world with my wife, I've cycled across the US, Australia and New Zealand, ran a couple of marathons and found the time to have two wonderful kids.

A Brief History of (My) Time

A rundown of my life and career over time.


1995

  • I buy my first computer. For no other reason than to write my Senior Thesis on the 'War of the Pacific'.
  • Graduate College with a Bachelors Degree in History - still waiting to put that to use.
  • Began career as a Video Game Tester and Software Support Representative at Legend Entertainment

1996

  • Acted as Roadie for Sleepy Time Trio on first US tour.
  • Worked as a Technical Support Staff member at Optical Technologies Group.

1997

  • Began life as a developer at Sensory Computing, Inc. (Which soon became Plateau Systems, Limited) developing Computer Based Training for Distance Learning projects.

1999

  • Took a two month long sabbatical to ride my bike on a solo, self-contained Tour of Australia.

2000

  • Became a full-time web professional as a User Interface Developer at CDG Interactive. Working on a variety of web sites and web applications for such client as: GEICO and Fannie Mae.

2001

  • Took a three month long sabbatical to ride my bike on a solo, self-contained Tour of the United States.

2002

  • Ran my first marathon - the Washington DC Marathon.
  • Web site for 'Brake the Cycle' for the USCCB.
  • Biked across Colorado with my future wife.

2005

  • Married my wonderful wife LeeAnne Adams after front-loading a honeymoon wherein we rode our bikes on a, self-contained cycling trip from Portland to San Francisco.

2007

  • Took a Gap Year trip around the world with my wife. Documented on our blog: Dave and Lolo Go There and Back.

2008

2012

2014
